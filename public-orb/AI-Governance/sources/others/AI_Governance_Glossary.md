# AI Governance Glossary

This glossary is a companion source for the public AI Governance demo. Each entry is short, declarative, and aligned to the vocabulary used in the framework documents and the supplementary checklists. Entries are intentionally packed into paragraph groups instead of per-line bullets so that the Builder's chunker emits chunks close to its target size — this is the same pattern used by the operational compendium and the launch readiness checklist.

## Roles, accountability, and approval

Intended use is the narrowly described set of tasks, inputs, users, and contexts for which an AI system is approved. Prohibited use is a use case that the deploying organization disallows for safety, legal, ethical, or contractual reasons; the system should refuse, route to a human, or block such requests entirely. High-risk use is a category defined by regulators, frameworks, or internal policy where heightened controls apply — including human oversight, formal evaluation, registration, and post-market monitoring. Use-case register is the authoritative inventory of AI use cases with risk classifications, owners, evidence pointers, and current status. Accountability matrix is a roles-and-responsibilities table that names the accountable executive, the technical owner, the privacy lead, the security lead, the evaluation lead, and other named roles for a given system. Approval authority is the named role that may approve or refuse a launch, a material change, or an exception; recorded in the policy registry. Separation of duties is a principle that prevents one role from both proposing and approving a sensitive change; required for production model promotion in mature programs.

Risk acceptance memo is a signed document recording the residual risk, the rationale, the conditions under which the acceptance holds, and the review date for re-evaluation. Residual risk is the risk that remains after primary and compensating controls have been applied; residual risk is accepted in writing by a named accountable person. Compensating control is a control that substitutes for a primary control that cannot be implemented, with documented justification and review schedule. Exception register is the structured log of approved policy exceptions, with the compensating control, the expiration date, and the approving authority. Operator training is the required training that an operator completes before being granted access to a sensitive AI workflow, with completion recorded. Governance committee is the cross-functional forum that approves use cases, accepts residual risk, resolves escalations, and reviews program effectiveness. AI risk register is the structured ledger of identified AI risks with owners, status, mitigations, and review dates. Operating cadence is the recurring rhythm of meetings, reviews, attestations, and reports that turns the policy layer into a working program.

## Human oversight, transparency, and user rights

Human-in-the-loop is a control design where a human reviewer participates in the decision before any consequential action is taken; the human can confirm, modify, or refuse the system suggestion. Human-on-the-loop is a control design where a human monitors system actions and can intervene, pause, or roll back, but does not approve each individual action. Override and challenge pathway is the documented and tested channel by which users may dispute or appeal an AI-affected outcome and receive human review. Right to a human review is honored within a documented service level for every challenge request. Refusal copy is the user-facing text used when the system declines a request; respectful, accurate, and localized. Disclosure of AI use is the user-facing notice informing the user they are interacting with an AI-enabled feature, including known limitations and human review options. Accessibility audit is a recurring check that user-facing surfaces meet accessibility standards across modalities. Localization governance ensures that policies, controls, and user-facing copy are consistent across the operating languages of the deployment. Multilingual evaluation is the practice of evaluating system performance separately across each deployed language to detect quality and safety regressions hidden by aggregate metrics. Equity review is a recurring assessment of differential impact across protected dimensions, with findings tracked to mitigation.

## Documentation, evidence, and traceability

Model card is a document that describes a model version, intended use, evaluation results, limitations, and known failure modes; referenced from the model inventory. System card is a document that describes the deployed system context including prompt templates, retrieval sources, tool registry, downstream automations, and user-facing disclosures. Evaluation report is a structured artifact containing the evaluation rubric, the test corpus identifiers, the protocol, the results, the thresholds, and the sign-off line. Traceability chain is the linked set of identifiers connecting policy, standards, procedures, controls, evidence, evaluation, monitoring, incidents, and approvals for a given system. Policy registry is the authoritative library of policy documents with version control, ownership, and change history. Quarterly attestation package is the bundle of evidence assembled and signed each quarter to demonstrate ongoing compliance with the governance program. External audit readiness folder is the maintained collection of artifacts that demonstrate program effectiveness to internal audit and external assessors on short notice. Board reporting cadence is the expected frequency at which the AI governance program reports to the board, with materials covering risk register, incidents, attestations, and forward plan.

## Data governance, provenance, and embeddings

Data provenance is the recorded origin and transformation history of a dataset, including sourcing, consent, licenses, transformations, and dependencies. Lineage diagram is a visual representation of data flows from source through transformations into model training, evaluation, and operational stores. Dataset inventory is the catalog of datasets used by an organization with metadata such as steward, license, refresh cadence, and sensitivity classification. Sensitive attribute handling policy is the documented approach to treating attributes that may trigger fairness, privacy, or anti-discrimination obligations. Right to erasure is the user right to request deletion of personal data, supported by an operational workflow that demonstrates completion within service-level commitments. Transfer impact assessment is a privacy-led review for cross-border data movement, evaluating legal basis, recipient safeguards, and residual risk. Embedding pipeline is the reproducible process for generating, storing, and refreshing vector embeddings, with version pinning to support rebuilds and audits. Retrieval allowlist is the vetted set of sources approved for retrieval into model context, with controls to prevent ingestion of unapproved or unsafe content. Tool registry is the catalog of external actions that a system is allowed to call, including parameters, permissions, rate limits, and audit logging.

## Safety, security, evaluation, and monitoring

Hallucination rate is the measured rate at which a generative system produces fabricated facts on a representative sample, measured against a curated ground-truth set. Refusal rate is the measured rate at which a system declines requests within a defined catalog of prohibited or restricted prompts. Drift is a change over time in model performance, input distribution, or output distribution that may reduce safety or quality; detected with drift monitors and benchmark refresh. Prompt injection is a class of attacks in which adversarial text instructs a model to ignore its operating instructions, exfiltrate data, or execute unintended actions; defenses include content provenance, instruction hierarchy, and isolation. Retrieval poisoning is a class of attacks in which adversarial content is placed inside retrievable corpora to manipulate model outputs; defenses include source allowlists, signature monitoring, and rebuild-on-detection workflows. Jailbreak resistance is a property of a deployed system that measures resistance to attempts to bypass safety policies through creative prompts, role-play, hypothetical framing, or obfuscation. Red-team review is a planned adversarial evaluation against a documented catalog of attacks; findings drive durable mitigations tracked to closure in the AI risk register. Sandboxing is the technical isolation of risky tool actions to limit blast radius if the system is misled or compromised. Rate limiting is a protective control that bounds request rates per user, per surface, or per tool to mitigate abuse, denial-of-service, or runaway costs. Output moderation is the pipeline that screens generated outputs against policy categories, blocks or rewrites violations, and logs decisions for audit.

Incident classification matrix is a reference table that maps observable events to severity tiers with corresponding response timelines, escalation contacts, and external notification triggers. Postmortem is a structured retrospective conducted after an incident or near-miss; captures timeline, contributing factors, systemic gaps, and durable mitigations with owners and due dates. Rollback runbook is the executable procedure for reverting a deployment to a previously approved state; includes pre-checks, sequencing, communications, and verification steps. Release gate is a defined checkpoint that a release candidate must pass before promotion, combining evaluation thresholds, evidence completeness, and approvals. Continuous monitoring is the recurring measurement of operational signals including quality, safety, drift, latency, cost, abuse indicators, and user feedback; drives alerts, dashboards, and periodic reviews. Attestation is a signed assertion by a named owner that a system meets defined criteria at a point in time; used quarterly, semiannually, or annually depending on risk tier. Vendor model upgrade review is a structured supply chain change process activated when an external model provider announces material behavioral changes. Version pinning is the configuration practice of locking a model or component to a specific version until a promotion review is complete. Staging rollout is the deployment pattern of releasing changes to a limited audience before broader promotion, with explicit promotion criteria and rollback rights. Telemetry retention policy is the defined window during which operational and incident telemetry must be retained for audit, with controls protecting sensitive content.
